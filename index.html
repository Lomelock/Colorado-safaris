Meet Colorado – A Young Maasai with a Dream as Big as a Mountain

The word "Colorado" refers to a great mountain in the United States — a symbol of strength, height, and boundless dreams. Here in Tanzania, "Colorado" is the name I go by — a nickname that represents who I am: a young Maasai man from a traditional community, carrying dreams as big as that mountain.

In my Maasai culture, wealth is traditionally measured by the number of livestock a person owns. Education is often seen as something distant or less valuable. But I believed in something different. I believed that education could change lives, and I chose a new path.

In 2014, I took a leap and began studying Tourism. It was a journey of dedication, hope, and purpose. After graduating, I began working in the tourism industry — guiding travelers from around the world, interpreting culture, and becoming a bridge between our rich traditions and the modern world.

Today, I’ve founded my own company — Colorado Tanzania Safaris — not just as a business, but as a mission: to share the natural beauty of Tanzania, the richness of the Maasai culture, and the power of education.

I call myself Colorado because my dreams are as high as a mountain, as strong as our traditions, and as expansive as the wild landscapes I guide visitors through.

My Mission

  • Offering high-quality, authentic safari experiences
  • Honoring and sharing Maasai culture with respect and depth
  • Inspiring youth, women, and children from my community to believe in education
  • Being a living example of how education can transform lives

Welcome to my journey — a journey of tourism, transformation, and hope.

Our Top Safari Experiences

Serengeti Expedition

Step into the heart of Africa’s most iconic wildlife haven. The Serengeti is world-renowned for the Great Migration—an epic journey of millions of wildebeest and zebras across vast plains. Expect dramatic predator encounters, endless horizons, and sunsets you’ll never forget.

Ngorongoro Crater Safari

A natural wonder unlike any other, the Ngorongoro Crater is a lush volcanic caldera teeming with wildlife—from black rhinos and elephants to lions and flamingos. It's also home to Maasai communities who have lived in harmony with wildlife for generations.

Lake Manyara Adventure

A hidden paradise known for its diverse landscapes—dense forests, alkaline lake shores, and open grasslands. Look for tree-climbing lions, elephants, and vibrant birdlife, including large flocks of flamingos.

Cultural Encounters

Tanzania's rich culture is as captivating as its landscapes. Meet the proud Maasai warriors, explore traditional Chaga villages, join in local dances, and learn about ancient customs and crafts. These authentic experiences connect you deeply to the land and its people, making your journey unforgettable.

Tarangire National Park

A peaceful escape filled with giant baobab trees and Tanzania’s largest elephant herds. Tarangire offers a more remote, off-the-beaten-path safari experience, rich in wildlife and serenity.

Discover Arusha, Tanzania

Arusha, known as the gateway to Tanzania’s renowned game reserves—including Serengeti, Ngorongoro, Tarangire, and Lake Manyara—is one of the country’s oldest towns. Originally a German military post in 1900, it has grown into Tanzania’s tourism hub with a population of around 400,000.

Located 1,390 meters above sea level, Arusha enjoys a mild climate year-round. Its strategic position along the Great North Road makes it the administrative center for the East African Community.

Arusha is perfect for souvenir shopping, offering Makonde carvings, vibrant Tinga Tinga paintings, and colorful batik textiles. It's also the only place on Earth where you’ll find Tanzanite—a rare, deep-blue gemstone discovered in 1967 in Mererani, just 40km away. Other gems like Ruby, Rhodolite, and Tourmaline are also sold in local shops.

Explore Arusha National Park

Arusha National Park is a gem in the northern circuit, offering a diverse landscape that includes grasslands, forests, and the stunning Ngurdoto Crater. The park is home to a wide variety of wildlife including giraffes, buffalo, and zebras, as well as many bird species, making it a birdwatcher’s paradise.

Scenic Views and Unique Wildlife

The park’s unique location at the base of Mount Meru provides breathtaking views, while the nearby Momella Lakes offer excellent opportunities for wildlife viewing and photography. Visitors can also experience a range of activities including game drives, walking safaris, and canoeing.

Activities and Experiences

  • Game Drives
  • Walking Safaris
  • Canoeing on Momella Lake
  • Birdwatching

Travel Time: Approx. 45 minutes by road from Arusha town.

Explore Serengeti National Park

Serengeti National Park is Africa’s most famous park due to the largest concentration of game on the continent and the migration of millions of wildebeest. The name 'Serengeti' comes from the Maasai language meaning ‘endless plains’, which is fitting for a park that spans nearly 15,000 km².

Experience the Great Migration

Each year, over a million wildebeest, along with hundreds of thousands of zebras and gazelles, journey across the Serengeti and into Kenya’s Masai Mara. Spanning about 1,000 kilometers, this dramatic migration offers incredible wildlife spectacles, especially at river crossings where predators and crocodiles add to the drama.

The migration can be witnessed year-round depending on rainfall and herd movements. From calving in the southern plains to dramatic river crossings in the west and north, Serengeti’s guides ensure unforgettable sightings throughout the cycle.

Wildlife Beyond the Migration

Home to more than 4,000 lions, 1,000 leopards, 225 cheetahs, and 3,500 hyenas, Serengeti’s predator population is unmatched. Visitors can also spot elephants, buffalo, and the rare black rhino, along with endangered species like pangolins and African wild dogs.

Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Serengeti’s landscapes include savannahs, forests, swamps, and rocky outcrops—each offering a different window into its ecological richness.

Activities and Travel Time

  • Hot air balloon safaris for a sunrise view of the plains

Travel Time:

  • From Arusha: ~5 hours by road
  • From Ngorongoro Crater: ~2 hours
  • From Lake Manyara: ~3.5 hours
  • From Tarangire: ~4 hours

Explore Ngorongoro Conservation Area

Ngorongoro, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and International Biosphere Reserve, lies about 190 km west of Arusha. The area includes the iconic Ngorongoro Crater, the world's largest intact volcanic caldera. Home to a rich diversity of wildlife, Ngorongoro is also inhabited by the Maasai people who live in harmony with nature and wildlife.

The Ngorongoro Crater

The crater’s floor is home to tens of thousands of animals, including wildebeest, zebra, gazelles, lions, hyenas, and jackals. Visitors might also spot rare species like the black rhino, cheetahs, and leopards. It’s one of the world’s most spectacular wildlife-viewing locations.

Beyond the Crater

The Ngorongoro Conservation Area extends beyond the crater, offering access to other attractions like Olduvai Gorge, Empakai Crater, and Mount Oldonyo Lengai. The area’s diverse landscapes and wildlife make it a must-visit destination for nature lovers.

Weather and Activities

  • Game drives around the crater floor
  • Hiking at Empakai Crater
  • Birdwatching at Lake Ndutu

Travel Time: Approx. 2 hours by road from Serengeti National Park.

Discover the Beauty of Lake Manyara National Park

Located at the base of the Great Rift Valley wall, Lake Manyara National Park offers a unique safari experience with breathtaking landscapes and abundant wildlife. The park's soda lake attracts flamingos, pelicans, and storks, while the dense forests are home to elephants and baboons. The park is also one of the few places in Africa where lions have been spotted lounging in trees.

Exploring the Rift Valley

Lake Manyara lies along the Rift Valley Escarpment, offering stunning views and a range of habitats for diverse wildlife. It's also a great spot for birdwatching, and you can encounter a variety of animals including giraffes, zebras, and buffaloes.

Activities and Experiences

  • Night game drives
  • Treetop canopy walks
  • Mountain biking tours
  • Cultural visits to Mto wa Mbu village

Travel Time: Approx. 2 hours by road from Arusha.

Discover Lake Natron and Oldonyo Lengai

Lake Natron, one of the most unique and striking natural wonders in Tanzania, is located in the northern part of the country, near the border with Kenya. This shallow, saline lake is framed by the stunning backdrop of the Great Rift Valley, with the towering presence of the active volcano **Oldonyo Lengai** looming over it. Known for its surreal landscapes and eerie beauty, Lake Natron is not only a vital ecosystem but also a place of mystery and intrigue.

The Mystical Lake Natron

Lake Natron is a caustic soda lake, which means its high levels of salinity and alkalinity make it uninhabitable for most species. Its red-tinted waters are the result of mineral-rich hot springs that feed into the lake, creating an otherworldly hue. Despite the harsh conditions, Lake Natron is an important breeding ground for millions of flamingos, particularly the Lesser Flamingo, which thrives in its alkaline waters.

The lake’s harsh environment, combined with its stunning beauty, makes it a popular destination for adventurous travelers seeking a unique experience. However, the lake’s alkalinity can make it hazardous, and care must be taken when visiting its shores. The mineral-rich mud and the salt flats surrounding the lake give the landscape a surreal, almost otherworldly feel.

Flamingos and Biodiversity

While most animals avoid the lake due to its caustic waters, the Lesser Flamingos are perfectly adapted to this extreme environment. They feed on the algae that grows in the alkaline waters, making it one of the few places in the world where these birds can thrive. During the breeding season, the shores of Lake Natron are filled with thousands of flamingos nesting in the mudflats, creating a stunning visual spectacle.

Aside from flamingos, the surrounding area is home to a variety of birds and other wildlife, including a large population of Lesser Kestrels, as well as various species of bat-eared foxes, dik-diks, and other small mammals. The surrounding environment, while harsh, supports a remarkable array of life that has adapted to this challenging ecosystem.

Oldonyo Lengai - The Mountain of God

Towering above Lake Natron is **Oldonyo Lengai**, an active volcano revered by the local Maasai people, who refer to it as “The Mountain of God.” At 2,878 meters, it is the only active volcano in the world that erupts natrocarbonatite lava, which is much cooler than the lava of other volcanoes. This type of lava creates a unique landscape on the slopes of Oldonyo Lengai, marked by black and white volcanic ash and mineral deposits.

Oldonyo Lengai is an awe-inspiring sight for those daring enough to trek up its slopes. The volcano’s eruptions are often small but can be dramatic, and its sulfur-rich landscape gives off a strong odor of brimstone, adding to the mystical feel of the area. Treks to the summit are not for the faint of heart, but for those who make it, the panoramic views of Lake Natron and the surrounding area are unparalleled.

Climbing Oldonyo Lengai

Climbing Oldonyo Lengai is an exhilarating challenge that attracts adventurous trekkers from around the world. The hike is typically done at night, as the cooler temperatures make the journey more bearable. The climb itself is strenuous, requiring a good level of fitness, but the reward is the spectacular sunrise view from the summit, offering a breathtaking panoramic view of the Rift Valley below.

Activities Around Lake Natron

  • Guided treks to the summit of Oldonyo Lengai
  • Birdwatching, particularly the Lesser Flamingo migration
  • Exploring the alkaline lake's unique formations
  • Photography of the surreal landscapes and flamingos
  • Cultural visits to the Maasai villages in the area

Travel Time: Approx. 4-5 hours by road from Arusha. Most visitors travel via the nearby town of **Mto wa Mbu**, which serves as a gateway to Lake Natron and Oldonyo Lengai.

Culture Encounters in Tanzania

Tanzania is more than its breathtaking landscapes and iconic wildlife—it's a vibrant tapestry of over 120 ethnic groups, each with distinct traditions, languages, and ways of life. A cultural encounter in Tanzania is a journey into the heart of its people, offering an intimate look at traditions passed down through generations. These experiences enrich any safari or adventure, allowing travelers to connect with the real soul of the country.

Moshi Cultural Tours & Coffee Plantation Visits

At the base of Mount Kilimanjaro lies the town of Moshi, surrounded by lush coffee plantations and home to the Chagga people. Cultural tours in Moshi include visits to traditional Chagga homes, where guests learn about local customs, food preparation, and historical cave shelters used during tribal wars. A walk through coffee farms introduces visitors to the entire coffee-making process—from bean to cup. You can even roast and brew your own cup of organic Kilimanjaro coffee!

Local Markets and Museums

Tanzania’s local markets are alive with color, aroma, and energy. In towns like Arusha, Moshi, and Stone Town (Zanzibar), you’ll find vibrant stalls selling everything from hand-woven baskets and Maasai beadwork to fresh spices and tropical fruits. Market visits provide an authentic glimpse into daily life, trade, and local crafts. Complement your experience with museum visits, such as the Arusha Declaration Museum or the Cultural Heritage Centre, which showcase artifacts, tribal history, and Tanzania’s path to independence.

Maasai Cultural Experiences

The iconic Maasai people are known for their deep-rooted traditions, distinctive red garments, and pastoral lifestyle. A visit to a Maasai boma (village) offers insight into their unique architecture, social structure, dance, and rituals. Engage with elders and warriors, watch traditional singing and jumping dances, and learn how the Maasai live in harmony with nature. These encounters are respectful and educational, emphasizing cultural exchange and preservation.

Indigenous Tribes: Hadzabe & Datoga

In the Lake Eyasi region, visitors can meet the Hadzabe—one of Africa’s last remaining hunter-gatherer tribes. Join them on a morning hunt using handmade bows and arrows, or gather wild tubers and fruits. The experience is raw and unfiltered, providing a rare window into a disappearing way of life. Nearby, the Datoga people are skilled blacksmiths and cattle herders who use traditional tools and forge weapons from recycled metals. These cultural visits are both humbling and unforgettable.

Cultural Activities You Can Experience:

  • Join a traditional dance ceremony with the Maasai or Chagga
  • Learn beadwork, pottery, or wood carving from local artisans
  • Take part in cooking traditional Tanzanian dishes
  • Attend storytelling sessions around a campfire
  • Visit schools and community projects to support local development

Cultural encounters are not just side activities—they are meaningful additions that allow travelers to understand the resilience, hospitality, and spirit of Tanzanian communities. They transform a trip into a truly immersive and memorable experience.

Best Regions for Cultural Tourism: Arusha, Moshi, Lake Eyasi, Monduli, Mto wa Mbu, Zanzibar, and Ngorongoro Highlands.
